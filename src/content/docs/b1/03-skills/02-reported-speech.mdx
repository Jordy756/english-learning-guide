Cuando cuentas lo que alguien dijo, no siempre usas sus palabras exactas. El **reported speech** (estilo indirecto) transforma las palabras originales. Piensa en ello como una función que recibe el mensaje original y lo procesa: cambia pronombres, tiempos verbales y referencias de tiempo.

Dominar el estilo indirecto es esencial para contar historias, reportar conversaciones y comunicar información de terceros.

---

## Direct vs Reported Speech

La diferencia fundamental entre citar directamente y reportar:

```javascript
const comparison = {
  direct: 'She said, "I am tired"',
  // Palabras exactas entre comillas
  reported: "She said (that) she was tired",
  // Transformado: pronombre + tiempo verbal
};
```

<CardGrid>
  <Card title="Estilo Directo">
    Palabras exactas con comillas
    
    *"I love pizza," he said*
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Estilo Indirecto">
    Palabras transformadas sin comillas
    
    *He said (that) he loved pizza*
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

<Aside type="tip">
La palabra **that** es opcional en reported speech: "He said he was tired" = "He said **that** he was tired"
</Aside>

## Cambio de Tiempos Verbales (Backshift)

Cuando el verbo de reporte está en pasado (said, told), los tiempos verbales "retroceden":

```javascript
const tenseBackshift = {
  present_simple: "past_simple",
  present_continuous: "past_continuous",
  present_perfect: "past_perfect",
  past_simple: "past_perfect",
  will: "would",
  can: "could",
  may: "might"
};
```

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="Presente → Pasado">
    ```javascript
    const presentToPast = {
      direct: '"I work here"',
      reported: "He said he worked there",
      // Simple present → Simple past
      
      direct2: '"I am working"',
      reported2: "He said he was working"
      // Present continuous → Past continuous
    };
    ```
    
    **Ejemplos:**
    - "I like coffee" → She said she liked coffee (Dijo que le gustaba el café)
    - "I'm studying" → He said he was studying (Dijo que estaba estudiando)
  </TabItem>
  
  <TabItem label="Pasado → Past Perfect">
    ```javascript
    const pastToPastPerfect = {
      direct: '"I saw the movie"',
      reported: "She said she had seen the movie",
      // Simple past → Past perfect
      
      direct2: '"I was sleeping"',
      reported2: "He said he had been sleeping"
      // Past continuous → Past perfect continuous
    };
    ```
    
    **Ejemplos:**
    - "I went home" → She said she had gone home (Dijo que se había ido a casa)
    - "I bought a car" → He said he had bought a car (Dijo que había comprado un carro)
  </TabItem>
  
  <TabItem label="Modales">
    ```javascript
    const modals = {
      will: "would",     // "I will call" → He said he would call
      can: "could",      // "I can swim" → She said she could swim
      may: "might",      // "I may go" → He said he might go
      must: "had to"     // "I must leave" → She said she had to leave
    };
    // would, could, might, should NO cambian
    ```
    
    **Ejemplos:**
    - "I will help" → He said he would help (Dijo que ayudaría)
    - "I can drive" → She said she could drive (Dijo que podía manejar)
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

<Aside type="caution">
**Would, could, should, might** ya están "retrocedidos" — no cambian en reported speech.
</Aside>

## Cambio de Pronombres

Los pronombres cambian según quién reporta:

```javascript
const pronounChanges = {
  I: "he/she",
  me: "him/her",
  my: "his/her",
  we: "they",
  us: "them",
  our: "their"
};
// Depende de quién habló originalmente
```

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="Ejemplos">
    ```javascript
    const examples = {
      original: '"I love my job"',
      // John habla de sí mismo
      reported: "John said he loved his job",
      // Cambia I→he, my→his
      
      original2: '"We need our tools"',
      reported2: "They said they needed their tools"
      // Cambia we→they, our→their
    };
    ```
  </TabItem>
  
  <TabItem label="Contexto">
    ```javascript
    const context = {
      // Si TÚ reportas lo que dijiste:
      direct: '"I am tired"',
      reported: "I said I was tired",
      // I no cambia porque TÚ lo dijiste
      
      // Si reportas lo que te dijeron:
      direct2: '"You are smart"',
      reported2: "She said I was smart"
      // You → I porque te lo dijeron a ti
    };
    ```
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

**Ejemplos:**
- "I need help" (Mary) → Mary said she needed help (Mary dijo que necesitaba ayuda)
- "We're coming" (friends) → They said they were coming (Dijeron que venían)

## Cambio de Tiempo y Lugar

Las referencias de tiempo y lugar también cambian:

```javascript
const timeChanges = {
  today: "that day",
  yesterday: "the day before",
  tomorrow: "the next day",
  now: "then",
  here: "there",
  this: "that",
  these: "those"
};
```

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="Tiempo">
    ```javascript
    const timeExamples = {
      direct: '"I will call you tomorrow"',
      reported: "He said he would call me the next day",
      
      direct2: '"I saw her yesterday"',
      reported2: "She said she had seen her the day before"
    };
    ```
    
    **Ejemplos:**
    - "I'm busy today" → He said he was busy that day (Dijo que estaba ocupado ese día)
    - "I'll do it now" → She said she would do it then (Dijo que lo haría entonces)
  </TabItem>
  
  <TabItem label="Lugar">
    ```javascript
    const placeExamples = {
      direct: '"I work here"',
      reported: "She said she worked there",
      
      direct2: '"I like this book"',
      reported2: "He said he liked that book"
    };
    ```
    
    **Ejemplos:**
    - "Come here" → She told me to go there (Me dijo que fuera allí)
    - "I want this one" → He said he wanted that one (Dijo que quería ese)
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

## Reportar Preguntas

Las preguntas en reported speech cambian su estructura:

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="Yes/No Questions">
    ```javascript
    const yesNoQuestions = {
      structure: "asked + if/whether + statement order",
      direct: '"Do you like coffee?"',
      reported: "She asked if I liked coffee",
      // Nota: orden de oración, NO de pregunta
    };
    ```
    
    **Ejemplos:**
    - "Are you coming?" → He asked if I was coming (Preguntó si yo venía)
    - "Did you see it?" → She asked whether I had seen it (Preguntó si lo había visto)
    - "Can you help?" → He asked if I could help (Preguntó si podía ayudar)
  </TabItem>
  
  <TabItem label="Wh- Questions">
    ```javascript
    const whQuestions = {
      structure: "asked + wh-word + statement order",
      direct: '"Where do you live?"',
      reported: "She asked where I lived",
      // SIN auxiliar do/does/did
    };
    ```
    
    **Ejemplos:**
    - "What is your name?" → He asked what my name was (Preguntó cuál era mi nombre)
    - "When did you arrive?" → She asked when I had arrived (Preguntó cuándo había llegado)
    - "Why are you sad?" → He asked why I was sad (Preguntó por qué estaba triste)
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

<Aside type="caution">
En reported questions, el orden es de **oración** (sujeto + verbo), **no** de pregunta. Y **no** llevan signo de interrogación.
</Aside>

## Reportar Órdenes y Peticiones

Usa **told/asked + object + to + infinitive**:

```javascript
const orders = {
  tell: "para órdenes",
  ask: "para peticiones",
  structure: "told/asked + person + (not) to + verb"
};
```

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="Órdenes (tell)">
    ```javascript
    const tellExamples = {
      direct: '"Close the door," he said',
      reported: "He told me to close the door",
      
      negative: '"Don\'t be late"',
      reported2: "She told him not to be late"
    };
    ```
    
    **Ejemplos:**
    - "Sit down" → She told me to sit down (Me dijo que me sentara)
    - "Don't touch that" → He told her not to touch that (Le dijo que no tocara eso)
  </TabItem>
  
  <TabItem label="Peticiones (ask)">
    ```javascript
    const askExamples = {
      direct: '"Please help me," she said',
      reported: "She asked me to help her",
      
      direct2: '"Could you wait?"',
      reported2: "He asked me to wait"
    };
    ```
    
    **Ejemplos:**
    - "Please call me" → She asked me to call her (Me pidió que la llamara)
    - "Don't leave yet" → He asked me not to leave yet (Me pidió que no me fuera todavía)
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

## Say vs Tell

La diferencia importante entre estos verbos de reporte:

```javascript
const sayVsTell = {
  say: {
    structure: "say (that) + clause",
    noObject: true,
    example: "She said (that) she was tired"
  },
  tell: {
    structure: "tell + person + (that) + clause",
    needsObject: true,
    example: "She told me (that) she was tired"
  }
};
```

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="Say">
    ```javascript
    const sayExamples = [
      "He said he was busy",      // correcto
      "He said that he was busy", // correcto
      // "He said me..." ← INCORRECTO
    ];
    // Say NO lleva objeto directo (persona)
    ```
    
    **Ejemplos:**
    - She said she would come (Dijo que vendría)
    - They said it was expensive (Dijeron que era caro)
  </TabItem>
  
  <TabItem label="Tell">
    ```javascript
    const tellExamples = [
      "He told me he was busy",    // correcto
      "He told us the truth",      // correcto
      // "He told that..." ← INCORRECTO
    ];
    // Tell SIEMPRE necesita objeto (a quién le dijeron)
    ```
    
    **Ejemplos:**
    - She told me she would come (Me dijo que vendría)
    - They told us it was expensive (Nos dijeron que era caro)
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

<Aside type="tip">
Recuerda: **Say** algo, **Tell** a alguien algo. "She said to me" también es correcto pero menos común.
</Aside>

## Cuándo NO Cambiar el Tiempo

A veces el backshift es opcional o no aplica:

```javascript
const noBackshift = {
  stillTrue: "La información sigue siendo verdad",
  recentPast: "Se dijo hace poco",
  examples: [
    // Verdad universal
    '"The earth is round"',
    'He said the earth is/was round',
    
    // Dicho hace poco
    '"I'm hungry" (hace 5 min)',
    'She said she is hungry'  // aún válido
  ]
};
```

**Ejemplos donde puedes mantener el tiempo:**
- "Paris is beautiful" → She said Paris is beautiful (Es verdad universal)
- "I have a headache" (dicho hace 1 minuto) → He said he has a headache (Aún tiene)

## Errores Comunes

<Aside type="danger">
**Mantener orden de pregunta en reported questions:**
- ~~She asked where was I going~~ → **She asked where I was going** (Preguntó adónde iba)
- ~~He asked did I like it~~ → **He asked if I liked it** (Preguntó si me gustó)
</Aside>

<Aside type="danger">
**Confundir say y tell:**
- ~~He said me that...~~ → **He told me that...** (Me dijo que...)
- ~~She told that...~~ → **She said that...** (Dijo que...)
</Aside>

<Aside type="danger">
**Olvidar cambiar los pronombres:**
- ~~She said "I am tired" → She said I was tired~~ → **She said she was tired** (Dijo que estaba cansada)
</Aside>

<Aside type="danger">
**Usar "to" con say para órdenes:**
- ~~He said me to go~~ → **He told me to go** (Me dijo que fuera)
- **Say** no se usa para reportar órdenes
</Aside>
